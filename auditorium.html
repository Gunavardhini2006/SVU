Sri Venkateswara University

Srinivasa Auditorium

The SVU Auditorium, also known as the Srinivasa Auditorium, is a landmark structure at Sri Venkateswara University, Tirupati. Designed by S.L. Chitale, it is renowned for its saddle-shaped hyperbolic paraboloid design inspired by the Tirumala Hills. This architectural marvel was a pioneering structure when it was constructed in the 1970s.

Key Features:

Unique Design: The auditorium's saddle shape reflects the surrounding Tirumala Hills, offering symbolic and structural significance.

Large Capacity: It can comfortably accommodate over 1500 students, making it ideal for major university events.

Thin Shell Construction: Built with a thin concrete shell only 4 inches thick, showcasing advanced engineering.

Pillar-Free Structure: The entire space is unobstructed, allowing clear views from all angles.

Modern Facilities: Equipped with high-quality audio-visual systems for lectures, seminars, and performances.
